# Professional Event Video Editing Standards

## Key Principles:
1. **Cut on the beat** - every transition synced with music rhythm
2. **Dynamic pacing** - mix of fast cuts (0.5-1s) and holds (2-3s)
3. **Hero moments** - slow-mo on key shots (speaker, reactions, emotions)
4. **Story arc** - Setup (arrival) → Build (networking) → Peak (speaker/key moment) → Resolution (community)
5. **Energy curve** - match video intensity to music energy

## Typical Structure for 30s empowerment event:
0-5s: HOOK - Best moment first (speaker or powerful group shot) with fade-in
5-12s: BUILD - Quick cuts showing diversity, activity, energy (1s per clip)
12-20s: PEAK - Slower, emotional moments (laughter, connection, 2-3s holds)
20-28s: OUTRO - Wide establishing shot, fade to black with event name/logo
28-30s: End card or logo

## Music Selection:
- BPM: 100-130 (energetic but not frantic)
- Style: Inspiring, uplifting, female empowerment vibe
- Must have clear beat markers for cuts
- Popular choices: indie pop, inspiring electronic, acoustic with build

## Transitions:
- Cross dissolve (0.3s) for smooth emotional moments
- Hard cuts on beat for energy
- Speed ramps (slow-mo) for hero shots
- No wipes/spins (dated)

## Color grading:
- Warm tones for intimate moments
- Consistent saturation across clips
- Slight vignette for focus
